### Formal Definition of Power

[[Work]] is related to **power** $P$, which is the [[Time]] rate of doing work. Differentiating the work expression with respect to time gives the instantaneous power.

$$
P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt} \int \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{F} \cdot \frac{d\mathbf{r}}{dt} = \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{v}
$$

Expressing this in Einstein notation:

$$
P = \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{v} = (F^i \mathbf{e}_i) \cdot (v^j \mathbf{e}_j) = F^i v^j \delta_{ij} = F^i v^i
$$

Thus, power is also elegantly expressed as:

$$
\boxed{P = F^i v^i}
$$
