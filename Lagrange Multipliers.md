## Formal Definition

**Lagrange Multipliers** are scalar variables introduced in constrained optimization problems to transform constrained extremum problems into unconstrained ones. The method embeds constraints into an augmented objective function (the Lagrangian), allowing optimization through standard calculus techniques. Each multiplier represents the sensitivity of the optimal objective value to changes in the corresponding constraint, providing both computational and interpretative insights into constrained systems.

- **1951:** Kuhn and Tucker formalize Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions
- **1960s:** Application expands to economics, operations research, and control theory

## Mathematical Formulation

### General Form

For optimization problem:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n} f(x) \\
&\text{subject to } g_i(x) = 0, \quad i = 1,\dots,m \\
&\quad \quad \quad h_j(x) \leq 0, \quad j = 1,\dots,p
\end{aligned}
$$

**Lagrangian function:**
$$
\mathcal{L}(x, \lambda, \mu) = f(x) + \sum_{i=1}^m \lambda_i g_i(x) + \sum_{j=1}^p \mu_j h_j(x)
$$

**First-order necessary conditions (KKT):**
1. **[[Stationarity]]:** $\nabla_x \mathcal{L}(x^*, \lambda^*, \mu^*) = 0$
2. **Primal feasibility:** $g_i(x^*) = 0, \quad h_j(x^*) \leq 0$
3. **Dual feasibility:** $\mu_j^* \geq 0$
4. **Complementary slackness:** $\mu_j^* h_j(x^*) = 0$

### Special Cases
1. **Equality constraints only:**
   $$
   \mathcal{L}(x, \lambda) = f(x) + \sum_{i=1}^m \lambda_i g_i(x)
   $$
   Conditions: $\nabla f(x^*) + \sum \lambda_i^* \nabla g_i(x^*) = 0$

2. **Single constraint:**
   $$
   \mathcal{L}(x, \lambda) = f(x) + \lambda g(x)
   $$

3. **Linear constraints:** For $Ax = b$ or $Ax \leq b$

## Fundamental Principles

### Geometric Interpretation

At optimum, gradient of objective is orthogonal to tangent space of feasible set:
$$
\nabla f(x^*) \in \text{span}\{\nabla g_i(x^*)\}
$$
For inequalities: $\nabla f(x^*)$ lies in cone generated by gradients of active constraints.

### Sensitivity Interpretation

Lagrange multiplier $\lambda_i^*$ gives rate of change of optimal value with respect to constraint:
$$
\frac{\partial f^*}{\partial b_i} = -\lambda_i^*
$$
where constraint is $g_i(x) = b_i$.

### Duality Theory

- **Lagrangian dual function:** $d(\lambda, \mu) = \inf_x \mathcal{L}(x, \lambda, \mu)$
- **Dual problem:** $\max_{\lambda \geq 0, \mu} d(\lambda, \mu)$
- **Weak duality:** $d(\lambda, \mu) \leq f(x)$ for feasible $x$
- **Strong duality:** Under conditions (convexity, constraint qualification), optimal values equal

## Theoretical Framework

### Constraint Qualifications

Conditions ensuring existence of multipliers:
1. **Linear Independence Constraint Qualification (LICQ):** Active constraint gradients linearly independent
2. **Mangasarian-Fromovitz Constraint Qualification (MFCQ):** Weaker than LICQ
3. **Slater's condition:** For convex problems, existence of strictly feasible point

### Second-Order Conditions

**Necessary conditions:** For local minimum, on tangent space $T$ of active constraints:
$$
y^T \nabla_{xx}^2 \mathcal{L}(x^*, \lambda^*, \mu^*) y \geq 0 \quad \forall y \in T
$$

**Sufficient conditions:** Strict inequality guarantees local minimum.

### Saddle Point Interpretation

$(x^*, \lambda^*, \mu^*)$ is saddle point:
$$
\mathcal{L}(x^*, \lambda, \mu) \leq \mathcal{L}(x^*, \lambda^*, \mu^*) \leq \mathcal{L}(x, \lambda^*, \mu^*)
$$
for $\mu \geq 0$ and feasible $x$.

4. **Interior-point methods:** Handle inequalities via barrier functions

## Derivation and Proof

### Derivation from First Principles

**Intuitive approach:** For equality constraint $g(x) = 0$, at optimum $x^*$, movement along constraint must not improve objective:
- Tangent direction $v$ satisfies $\nabla g(x^*)^T v = 0$
- For optimality: $\nabla f(x^*)^T v = 0$
- Thus $\nabla f(x^*)$ parallel to $\nabla g(x^*)$: $\nabla f(x^*) + \lambda \nabla g(x^*) = 0$

**Formal derivation via implicit function theorem:**
1. Assume LICQ holds at $x^*$
2. Partition variables: $x = (y, z)$ where $\nabla_z g$ nonsingular
3. By implicit function theorem: $z = \phi(y)$ near $x^*$ with $g(y, \phi(y)) = 0$
4. Unconstrained problem in $y$: $\min_y f(y, \phi(y))$
5. First-order condition: $\nabla_y f + \nabla_z f \cdot \nabla_y \phi = 0$
6. From implicit differentiation: $\nabla_y \phi = -[\nabla_z g]^{-1} \nabla_y g$
7. Let $\lambda^T = \nabla_z f [\nabla_z g]^{-1}$
8. Obtain: $\nabla f + \lambda^T \nabla g = 0$

### KKT Conditions Derivation

**Via Fritz John conditions:** Consider problem with inequalities
**Fritz John conditions:** Exist multipliers $(\lambda_0, \lambda, \mu) \neq 0$ with $\lambda_0 \geq 0, \mu \geq 0$ such that:
- $\lambda_0 \nabla f(x^*) + \sum \lambda_i \nabla g_i(x^*) + \sum \mu_j \nabla h_j(x^*) = 0$
- $\mu_j h_j(x^*) = 0$

**Under constraint qualification:** Can take $\lambda_0 = 1$ (KKT conditions).

## Physical Interpretation

### Mechanics Interpretation

In classical mechanics:
- **Objective function:** [[Action]] integral $S = \int L dt$
- **Constraints:** Holonomic constraints $g(q,t) = 0$
- **Lagrange multipliers:** Constraint forces (tension, normal forces)
- **Equations of motion:** [[Euler-Lagrange Equations]] with multipliers

### Economics Interpretation

In utility maximization:
- **Objective:** Utility function $U(x)$
- **Constraint:** Budget constraint $p^T x \leq I$
- **Lagrange multiplier:** Marginal utility of income (shadow price)
- **Interpretation:** Value of relaxing constraint by one unit

### Engineering Interpretation

In structural optimization:
- **Objective:** Weight or cost
- **Constraints:** Stress, displacement, frequency limits
- **Multipliers:** Sensitivity of optimal design to constraint limits

## Implementation Considerations

### Numerical Methods

**Primal-dual interior-point method:**
```python
import numpy as np
from scipy.optimize import minimize

def lagrange_multiplier_example():
    # Example: min f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 subject to x + y = 1
    
    def objective(x):
        return x[0]**2 + x[1]**2
    
    # Equality constraint: x + y - 1 = 0
    cons = ({'type': 'eq', 'fun': lambda x: x[0] + x[1] - 1})
    
    # Initial guess
    x0 = np.array([0.5, 0.5])
    
    # Solve using SLSQP (implements Lagrange multiplier method)
    result = minimize(objective, x0, constraints=cons, method='SLSQP')
    
    print(f"Optimal x: {result.x}")
    print(f"Objective value: {result.fun}")
    
    # Get Lagrange multiplier (available in some methods)
    if hasattr(result, 'v'):
        print(f"Lagrange multiplier: {result.v[0]}")
    
    return result

# Analytic solution for comparison
def analytic_solution():
    # Lagrangian: L = x^2 + y^2 + λ(x + y - 1)
    # ∂L/∂x = 2x + λ = 0 => x = -λ/2
    # ∂L/∂y = 2y + λ = 0 => y = -λ/2
    # Constraint: x + y = 1 => -λ/2 - λ/2 = 1 => -λ = 1 => λ = -1
    # Then x = 0.5, y = 0.5, f = 0.5
    
    λ = -1.0
    x = 0.5
    y = 0.5
    f_val = 0.5
    
    return x, y, λ, f_val
```

## Advantages and Limitations

### Strengths
1. **Systematic approach:** Transforms constrained to unconstrained problems
2. **Sensitivity information:** Multipliers provide shadow prices
3. **Dual formulation:** Often simpler, especially for convex problems
4. **Theoretical foundation:** Well-understood optimality conditions
5. **Computational efficiency:** Many specialized algorithms available

### Weaknesses
1. **Local solutions:** Only guarantees local optimality for non-convex problems
2. **Constraint qualification:** May fail to hold (e.g., at cusps)
3. **Duality gap:** For non-convex problems, primal and dual may differ
4. **Numerical issues:** Ill-conditioning, slow convergence
5. **Complementary slackness:** Non-smoothness at constraint boundaries

### Validity Range
- **Well-defined:** Smooth problems with regular constraints (LICQ holds)
- **Problematic:** Non-smooth objectives, degenerate constraints
- **Inapplicable:** Discrete optimization (requires integer programming)

## Advanced Topics

### Non-Smooth Optimization
- **Subgradients:** Generalized gradients for non-differentiable functions
- **Bundle methods:** Handle non-smoothness via subgradient information
- **Proximal methods:** Regularization for non-smooth terms

### Stochastic Optimization
- **Chance constraints:** Constraints holding with probability
- **Stochastic multipliers:** Random Lagrange multipliers
- **Scenario-based methods:** Sample average approximation

### Semi-Infinite Programming
Constraints indexed by continuous parameter:
$$
\min_x f(x) \text{ s.t. } g(x,t) \leq 0 \ \forall t \in T
$$
Multipliers become measures on $T$.

### Multi-Objective Optimization
- **Pareto optimality:** No single optimal, trade-off surface
- **Weighted sum method:** Scalarization using weights as parameters
- **ε-constraint method:** Treat objectives as constraints

## Special Cases and Examples

### Simple Analytical Example
**Problem:** $\min x^2 + y^2$ subject to $x + y = 1$

**Solution:**
- Lagrangian: $\mathcal{L} = x^2 + y^2 + \lambda(x + y - 1)$
- Conditions: $2x + \lambda = 0$, $2y + \lambda = 0$, $x + y = 1$
- Solve: $x = y = 0.5$, $\lambda = -1$, $f^* = 0.5$

### Inequality Constraint Example
**Problem:** $\min x^2 + y^2$ subject to $x + y \geq 1$

**KKT conditions:**
- $\mathcal{L} = x^2 + y^2 + \mu(1 - x - y)$
- [[Stationarity]]: $2x - \mu = 0$, $2y - \mu = 0$
- Primal: $x + y \geq 1$
- Dual: $\mu \geq 0$
- Complementary slackness: $\mu(1 - x - y) = 0$

**Solution:** Active constraint: $x + y = 1$, so same as equality case.

### Economic Example: Utility Maximization
**Problem:** $\max U(x,y) = x^\alpha y^{1-\alpha}$ subject to $p_x x + p_y y = I$

**Lagrangian:** $\mathcal{L} = x^\alpha y^{1-\alpha} + \lambda(I - p_x x - p_y y)$

**Solution:**
- $x^* = \frac{\alpha I}{p_x}$, $y^* = \frac{(1-\alpha)I}{p_y}$
- $\lambda^* = \alpha^\alpha (1-\alpha)^{1-\alpha} I^{\alpha-1} p_x^{-\alpha} p_y^{-(1-\alpha)}$
- $\lambda$ = marginal utility of income

## Implementation Examples

### Comprehensive Python Implementation

```python
import numpy as np
import sympy as sp
from scipy.optimize import minimize, Bounds, LinearConstraint, NonlinearConstraint
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

class LagrangeMultiplierSolver:
    """General Lagrange multiplier solver for optimization problems"""
    
    def __init__(self):
        pass
    
    def solve_equality_constrained(self, f, g, x0, method='SLSQP'):
        """
        Solve min f(x) subject to g(x) = 0
        
        Parameters
        ----------
        f : callable
            Objective function f(x)
        g : callable or list of callables
            Equality constraint(s) g_i(x) = 0
        x0 : array
            Initial guess
        method : str
            Optimization method ('SLSQP', 'trust-constr', etc.)
            
        Returns
        -------
        result : dict
            Solution with x, fval, lambda, etc.
        """
        # Convert single constraint to list
        if callable(g):
            g = [g]
        
        # Define constraints for scipy
        constraints = []
        for gi in g:
            constraints.append({'type': 'eq', 'fun': gi})
        
        # Solve optimization
        result = minimize(f, x0, constraints=constraints, method=method)
        
        # For methods that return multipliers
        if hasattr(result, 'v'):
            multipliers = result.v
        else:
            # Estimate multipliers numerically
            multipliers = self._estimate_multipliers(f, g, result.x)
        
        return {
            'x': result.x,
            'fval': result.fun,
            'success': result.success,
            'multipliers': multipliers,
            'message': result.message
        }
    
    def solve_inequality_constrained(self, f, h, x0, method='SLSQP'):
        """
        Solve min f(x) subject to h(x) <= 0
        
        Parameters
        ----------
        f : callable
            Objective function
        h : callable or list
            Inequality constraint(s) h_i(x) <= 0
        x0 : array
            Initial guess
        method : str
            Optimization method
            
        Returns
        -------
        result : dict
            Solution with x, fval, multipliers, etc.
        """
        if callable(h):
            h = [h]
        
        constraints = []
        for hi in h:
            constraints.append({'type': 'ineq', 'fun': lambda x, hi=hi: -hi(x)})
        
        result = minimize(f, x0, constraints=constraints, method=method)
        
        # Get multipliers if available
        if hasattr(result, 'v'):
            multipliers = result.v
        else:
            multipliers = self._estimate_multipliers_inequality(f, h, result.x)
        
        return {
            'x': result.x,
            'fval': result.fun,
            'success': result.success,
            'multipliers': multipliers,
            'active_constraints': self._identify_active(h, result.x),
            'message': result.message
        }
    
    def _estimate_multipliers(self, f, g, x_opt, epsilon=1e-5):
        """
        Estimate Lagrange multipliers numerically using finite differences
        
        For equality constraints, solve linear system:
        ∇f + ∑ λ_i ∇g_i = 0
        """
        n = len(x_opt)
        m = len(g)
        
        if m == 0:
            return np.array([])
        
        # Approximate gradients using finite differences
        def approx_grad(func, x):
            grad = np.zeros(n)
            for i in range(n):
                x_plus = x.copy()
                x_minus = x.copy()
                x_plus[i] += epsilon
                x_minus[i] -= epsilon
                grad[i] = (func(x_plus) - func(x_minus)) / (2*epsilon)
            return grad
        
        # Gradient of objective
        grad_f = approx_grad(f, x_opt)
        
        # Gradients of constraints
        grad_g = np.zeros((m, n))
        for i in range(m):
            grad_g[i, :] = approx_grad(g[i], x_opt)
        
        # Solve for λ: grad_f + λ^T grad_g = 0
        # Equivalent to: grad_g λ = -grad_f
        try:
            # Use least squares in case of redundancy
            lambdas, residuals, rank, s = np.linalg.lstsq(grad_g.T, -grad_f, rcond=None)
            return lambdas
        except np.linalg.LinAlgError:
            return np.zeros(m)
    
    def _estimate_multipliers_inequality(self, f, h, x_opt, epsilon=1e-5):
        """Estimate KKT multipliers for inequality constraints"""
        m = len(h)
        lambdas = np.zeros(m)
        
        # Only estimate for active constraints
        active = self._identify_active(h, x_opt)
        
        if len(active) > 0:
            # Use only active constraints for estimation
            active_h = [h[i] for i in active]
            active_lambdas = self._estimate_multipliers(f, active_h, x_opt, epsilon)
            
            # Place back in full multiplier vector
            for idx, a_idx in enumerate(active):
                lambdas[a_idx] = active_lambdas[idx]
        
        # Ensure non-negativity for inequality multipliers
        lambdas = np.maximum(lambdas, 0)
        
        return lambdas
    
    def _identify_active(self, h, x, tol=1e-6):
        """Identify active inequality constraints (h_i(x) ≈ 0)"""
        active = []
        for i, hi in enumerate(h):
            if abs(hi(x)) < tol:
                active.append(i)
        return active
    
    def analytic_solution(self, f_str, g_strs, h_strs=None, vars_str='x y'):
        """
        Find analytic solution using symbolic computation
        
        Parameters
        ----------
        f_str : str
            Objective function as string
        g_strs : list of str
            Equality constraints as strings
        h_strs : list of str
            Inequality constraints as strings
        vars_str : str
            Variable names separated by spaces
            
        Returns
        -------
        solutions : list
            List of candidate solutions (x, λ, μ)
        """
        # Parse variables
        var_names = vars_str.split()
        vars_sym = sp.symbols(vars_str)
        
        # Parse objective and constraints
        f_sym = sp.sympify(f_str)
        g_syms = [sp.sympify(g_str) for g_str in g_strs] if g_strs else []
        h_syms = [sp.sympify(h_str) for h_str in h_strs] if h_strs else []
        
        # Create Lagrangian
        λ_syms = sp.symbols(f'λ:{len(g_syms)}') if g_syms else []
        μ_syms = sp.symbols(f'μ:{len(h_syms)}') if h_syms else []
        
        L = f_sym
        for i, g in enumerate(g_syms):
            L += λ_syms[i] * g
        for j, h in enumerate(h_syms):
            L += μ_syms[j] * h
        
        # First-order conditions
        equations = []
        
        # [[Stationarity]]: ∂L/∂x_i = 0
        for var in vars_sym:
            equations.append(sp.diff(L, var))
        
        # Equality constraints: g_i = 0
        equations.extend(g_syms)
        
        # Inequality constraints: h_j <= 0
        # For KKT, we consider cases where constraints are active (h_j = 0)
        # and inactive (μ_j = 0)
        
        # Complementary slackness: μ_j * h_j = 0
        for j, h in enumerate(h_syms):
            equations.append(μ_syms[j] * h)
        
        # Dual feasibility: μ_j >= 0 (handled separately)
        
        # Solve system
        all_vars = list(vars_sym) + list(λ_syms) + list(μ_syms)
        solutions = sp.solve(equations, all_vars, dict=True)
        
        # Filter by dual feasibility (μ_j >= 0)
        feasible_solutions = []
        for sol in solutions:
            feasible = True
            for j, μ_sym in enumerate(μ_syms):
                if μ_sym in sol:
                    if sol[μ_sym] < 0:  # Negative multiplier for inequality
                        feasible = False
                        break
            if feasible:
                feasible_solutions.append(sol)
        
        return feasible_solutions

        g_strs=['x + y - 1'],
        vars_str='x y'
    )
    
    print("\nAnalytical solutions:")
    for sol in solutions:
        print(f"  x = {sol[sp.Symbol('x')]:.6f}, y = {sol[sp.Symbol('y')]:.6f}, λ = {sol[sp.Symbol('λ0')]:.6f}")
    
    # Visualization
    x_vals = np.linspace(-1, 2, 100)
    y_vals = np.linspace(-1, 2, 100)
    X, Y = np.meshgrid(x_vals, y_vals)
    Z = X**2 + Y**2
    
    plt.figure(figsize=(10, 8))
    
    # Contour plot of objective
    contour = plt.contour(X, Y, Z, levels=20, cmap='viridis', alpha=0.7)
    plt.colorbar(contour, label='f(x,y) = x² + y²')
    
    # Constraint line
    constraint_y = 1 - x_vals
    plt.plot(x_vals, constraint_y, 'r-', linewidth=2, label='Constraint: x + y = 1')
    
    # Optimal point
    x_opt = result['x'][0]
    y_opt = result['x'][1]
    plt.plot(x_opt, y_opt, 'ro', markersize=10, label=f'Optimum ({x_opt:.2f}, {y_opt:.2f})')
    
    # Gradient vectors at optimum
    grad_f = np.array([2*x_opt, 2*y_opt])
    grad_g = np.array([1, 1])
    lambda_opt = result['multipliers'][0]
    
    plt.arrow(x_opt, y_opt, grad_f[0]/10, grad_f[1]/10, 
              head_width=0.05, head_length=0.1, fc='blue', ec='blue', 
              label='∇f(x*)')
    plt.arrow(x_opt, y_opt, grad_g[0]/10, grad_g[1]/10, 
              head_width=0.05, head_length=0.1, fc='red', ec='red',
              label='∇g(x*)')
    plt.arrow(x_opt, y_opt, -lambda_opt*grad_g[0]/10, -lambda_opt*grad_g[1]/10,
              head_width=0.05, head_length=0.1, fc='green', ec='green',
              label='-λ∇g(x*)')
    
    plt.xlabel('x')
    plt.ylabel('y')
    plt.title('Equality Constrained Optimization: min x² + y² s.t. x + y = 1')
    plt.legend()
    plt.grid(alpha=0.3)
    plt.axis('equal')
    plt.show()
    
    return result

def example_inequality_constrained():
    """Example: min x^2 + y^2 subject to x + y >= 1"""
    print("\n" + "="*60)
    print("Example 2: Inequality Constrained Optimization")
    print("="*60)
    
    solver = LagrangeMultiplierSolver()
    
    # Define problem
    f = lambda x: x[0]**2 + x[1]**2
    h = lambda x: 1 - x[0] - x[1]  # h(x) <= 0 equivalent to x + y >= 1
    
    # Numerical solution
    result = solver.solve_inequality_constrained(f, [h], x0=[0.0, 0.0])
    
    print("Numerical solution:")
    print(f"  x = {result['x'][0]:.6f}, y = {result['x'][1]:.6f}")
    print(f"  f(x,y) = {result['fval']:.6f}")
    print(f"  μ = {result['multipliers'][0]:.6f}")
    print(f"  Constraint active: {0 in result['active_constraints']}")
    
    # Analytical KKT analysis
    print("\nKKT conditions analysis:")
    print("  [[Stationarity]]: 2x - μ = 0, 2y - μ = 0")
    print("  Primal feasibility: x + y >= 1")
    print("  Dual feasibility: μ >= 0")
    print("  Complementary slackness: μ(1 - x - y) = 0")
    
    # Two cases:
    print("\nCase 1: μ = 0 (constraint inactive)")
    print("  Then x = 0, y = 0 from [[stationarity]]")
    print("  But x + y = 0 < 1, violates constraint")
    print("  So case 1 infeasible")
    
    print("\nCase 2: 1 - x - y = 0 (constraint active)")
    print("  Then same as equality case: x = y = 0.5, μ = 1")
    print("  All conditions satisfied")
    
    # Visualization
    x_vals = np.linspace(-0.5, 1.5, 100)
    y_vals = np.linspace(-0.5, 1.5, 100)
    X, Y = np.meshgrid(x_vals, y_vals)
    Z = X**2 + Y**2
    
    plt.figure(figsize=(10, 8))
    
    # Contour plot
    contour = plt.contour(X, Y, Z, levels=20, cmap='viridis', alpha=0.7)
    plt.colorbar(contour, label='f(x,y) = x² + y²')
    
    # Feasible region
    feasible = X + Y >= 1
    plt.contourf(X, Y, feasible, levels=[0.5, 1.5], colors=['lightgray'], alpha=0.3)
    
    # Constraint boundary
    constraint_y = 1 - x_vals
    plt.plot(x_vals, constraint_y, 'r-', linewidth=2, label='Constraint: x + y = 1')
    
    # Fill feasible region
    plt.fill_between(x_vals, constraint_y, 1.5, color='lightgray', alpha=0.3, label='Feasible region')
    
    # Optimal point
    x_opt = result['x'][0]
    y_opt = result['x'][1]
    plt.plot(x_opt, y_opt, 'ro', markersize=10, label=f'Optimum ({x_opt:.2f}, {y_opt:.2f})')
    
    # Show unconstrained minimum
    plt.plot(0, 0, 'bo', markersize=8, label='Unconstrained minimum (0,0)')
    
    plt.xlabel('x')
    plt.ylabel('y')
    plt.title('Inequality Constrained Optimization: min x² + y² s.t. x + y ≥ 1')
    plt.legend()
    plt.grid(alpha=0.3)
    plt.axis('equal')
    plt.show()
    
    return result

def example_economic():
    """Example: Utility maximization with budget constraint"""
    print("\n" + "="*60)
    print("Example 3: Economic Application - Utility Maximization")
    print("="*60)
    
    # Parameters
    alpha = 0.7  # Preference for good x
    px = 2.0     # Price of good x
    py = 3.0     # Price of good y
    I = 100.0    # Income
    
    # Cobb-Douglas utility: U(x,y) = x^α y^(1-α)
    # Budget constraint: px*x + py*y = I
    
    # Analytic solution
    x_star = alpha * I / px
    y_star = (1 - alpha) * I / py
    U_star = x_star**alpha * y_star**(1-alpha)
    
    # Lagrange multiplier (marginal utility of income)
    λ_star = alpha**alpha * (1-alpha)**(1-alpha) * I**(alpha-1) * px**(-alpha) * py**(-(1-alpha))
    
    print("Consumer choice problem:")
    print(f"  Utility: U(x,y) = x^{alpha} * y^{1-alpha}")
    print(f"  Budget: {px}x + {py}y = {I}")
    print(f"\nAnalytic solution:")
    print(f"  x* = {x_star:.2f} units of good x")
    print(f"  y* = {y_star:.2f} units of good y")
    print(f"  U* = {U_star:.2f} utils")
    print(f"  λ* = {λ_star:.6f} (marginal utility of income)")
    print(f"  Interpretation: Increasing income by $1 increases utility by {λ_star:.4f} utils")
    
    # Verify with numerical optimization
    solver = LagrangeMultiplierSolver()
    
    # Negative utility for minimization
    f = lambda x: -(x[0]**alpha * x[1]**(1-alpha))
    g = lambda x: px*x[0] + py*x[1] - I
    
    result = solver.solve_equality_constrained(f, g, x0=[x_star, y_star])
    
    print(f"\nNumerical verification:")
    print(f"  x* = {result['x'][0]:.6f}, y* = {result['x'][1]:.6f}")
    print(f"  U* = {-result['fval']:.6f}")
    print(f"  λ* = {result['multipliers'][0]:.6f}")
    
    # Sensitivity analysis
    print("\nSensitivity analysis:")
    print("  Changing px from 2.0 to 2.1:")
    px_new = 2.1
    x_new = alpha * I / px_new
    y_new = (1 - alpha) * I / py
    U_new = x_new**alpha * y_new**(1-alpha)
    ΔU = U_new - U_star
    Δpx = 0.1
    predicted_ΔU = -λ_star * x_star * Δpx
    print(f"    Actual ΔU = {ΔU:.6f}")
    print(f"    Predicted ΔU using λ = {predicted_ΔU:.6f}")
    
    # Visualization
    x_vals = np.linspace(0, 100, 100)
    y_vals_budget = (I - px*x_vals) / py
    
    # Indifference curves
    U_levels = [U_star*0.5, U_star, U_star*1.5]
    
    plt.figure(figsize=(12, 8))
    
    # Budget line
    plt.plot(x_vals, y_vals_budget, 'r-', linewidth=2, label=f'Budget: {px}x + {py}y = {I}')
    plt.fill_between(x_vals, 0, y_vals_budget, alpha=0.1, color='red', label='Affordable bundles')
    
    # Indifference curves
    for U_level in U_levels:
        y_vals_ic = (U_level / (x_vals**alpha))**(1/(1-alpha))
        y_vals_ic = np.nan_to_num(y_vals_ic, posinf=0)
        plt.plot(x_vals, y_vals_ic, 'b--', alpha=0.5)
        plt.text(x_vals[-10], y_vals_ic[-10], f'U={U_level:.0f}', fontsize=9)
    
    # Optimal point
    plt.plot(x_star, y_star, 'ro', markersize=10, label=f'Optimum ({x_star:.1f}, {y_star:.1f})')
    
    # Tangent condition visualization
    # Slope of indifference curve at optimum: -MRS = -αy/((1-α)x)
    mrs = alpha * y_star / ((1 - alpha) * x_star)
    # Slope of budget line: -px/py
    budget_slope = -px/py
    
    # Show tangency
    dx = 10
    dy_ic = mrs * dx
    dy_budget = budget_slope * dx
    
    plt.arrow(x_star, y_star, dx, dy_ic, head_width=2, head_length=2, 
              fc='blue', ec='blue', alpha=0.5, label='Indifference curve slope')
    plt.arrow(x_star, y_star, dx, dy_budget, head_width=2, head_length=2,
              fc='red', ec='red', alpha=0.5, label='Budget line slope')
    
    plt.xlabel('Good x (quantity)')
    plt.ylabel('Good y (quantity)')
    plt.title(f'Utility Maximization: U(x,y)=x^{alpha}y^{1-alpha} with Budget Constraint')
    plt.legend()
    plt.grid(alpha=0.3)
    plt.xlim(0, 60)
    plt.ylim(0, 40)
    plt.show()
    
    return result

def example_multiple_constraints():
    """Example with multiple constraints"""
    print("\n" + "="*60)
    print("Example 4: Multiple Constraints")
    print("="*60)
    
    # Problem: min f(x,y) = (x-4)^2 + (y-4)^2
    # Subject to: x + y <= 6, x >= 0, y >= 0
    
    solver = LagrangeMultiplierSolver()
    
    # Define using scipy's constraint objects
    f = lambda x: (x[0]-4)**2 + (x[1]-4)**2
    
    # Constraints: h1(x) = x + y - 6 <= 0, h2(x) = -x <= 0, h3(x) = -y <= 0
    constraints = [
        {'type': 'ineq', 'fun': lambda x: 6 - x[0] - x[1]},  # x + y <= 6
        {'type': 'ineq', 'fun': lambda x: x[0]},             # x >= 0
        {'type': 'ineq', 'fun': lambda x: x[1]}              # y >= 0
    ]
    
    # Solve
    result = minimize(f, [0, 0], constraints=constraints, method='SLSQP')
    
    print("Problem: min (x-4)² + (y-4)²")
    print("Subject to: x + y ≤ 6, x ≥ 0, y ≥ 0")
    print(f"\nSolution: x = {result.x[0]:.6f}, y = {result.x[1]:.6f}")
    print(f"Objective: f = {result.fun:.6f}")
    
    # Analytical KKT analysis
    print("\nKKT analysis:")
    print("Active constraints at optimum:")
    
    # Check which constraints are active
    active = []
    if abs(result.x[0] + result.x[1] - 6) < 1e-6:
        active.append("x + y = 6")
    if abs(result.x[0]) < 1e-6:
        active.append("x = 0")
    if abs(result.x[1]) < 1e-6:
        active.append("y = 0")
    
    for constr in active:
        print(f"  {constr}")
    
    # The solution should be at the intersection of active constraints
    # In this case, likely x + y = 6 is active
    
    # Visualization
    x_vals = np.linspace(-1, 7, 100)
    y_vals = np.linspace(-1, 7, 100)
    X, Y = np.meshgrid(x_vals, y_vals)
    Z = (X-4)**2 + (Y-4)**2
    
    plt.figure(figsize=(10, 8))
    
    # Contour plot
    contour = plt.contour(X, Y, Z, levels=20, cmap='viridis', alpha=0.7)
    plt.colorbar(contour, label='f(x,y) = (x-4)² + (y-4)²')
    
    # Feasible region
    feasible = (X + Y <= 6) & (X >= 0) & (Y >= 0)
    plt.contourf(X, Y, feasible, levels=[0.5, 1.5], colors=['lightgray'], alpha=0.3)
    
    # Constraints
    # x + y = 6
    plt.plot(x_vals, 6 - x_vals, 'r-', linewidth=2, label='x + y = 6')
    # x = 0
    plt.axvline(x=0, color='g', linestyle='-', linewidth=2, label='x = 0')
    # y = 0
    plt.axhline(y=0, color='b', linestyle='-', linewidth=2, label='y = 0')
    
    # Fill feasible region
    plt.fill_between(x_vals, 0, 6 - x_vals, where=(x_vals>=0) & (6-x_vals>=0), 
                     color='lightgray', alpha=0.3)
    
    # Optimal point
    plt.plot(result.x[0], result.x[1], 'ro', markersize=10, 
             label=f'Optimum ({result.x[0]:.2f}, {result.x[1]:.2f})')
    
    # Unconstrained minimum
    plt.plot(4, 4, 'bo', markersize=8, label='Unconstrained minimum (4,4)')
    
    plt.xlabel('x')
    plt.ylabel('y')
    plt.title('Multiple Constraints: min (x-4)² + (y-4)² s.t. x+y≤6, x≥0, y≥0')
    plt.legend()
    plt.grid(alpha=0.3)
    plt.axis('equal')
    plt.xlim(-1, 7)
    plt.ylim(-1, 7)
    plt.show()
    
    return result
```
