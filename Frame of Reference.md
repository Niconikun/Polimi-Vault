### Formal Definition of a Frame of Reference

A **frame of reference** is a theoretical, physical construct consisting of:

1. **A [[Coordinate System]]:** A set of rules for assigning numerical coordinates (e.g., x, y, z) to points in space to specify their position.
    
2. **A Set of Clocks:** A means of assigning a [[Time]] coordinate to events.
    
3. **A Physical Observer or [[Rigid Body]]:** The coordinate system and clocks are conceived as being attached to a physical object or observer, whose state of motion (velocity, acceleration, [[Rotation]]) defines the frame's properties.
    

The primary purpose of a frame of reference is to **enable the measurement and mathematical description of the position and time of events** (like "the ball hits the ground") in a consistent and unambiguous way. It is the stage upon which the laws of physics are tested and formulated.
