### **Earth-Centered Inertial Frame (ECI)**

**Formal Definition:**
The **Earth-Centered Inertial Frame** is an [[Inertial Frame of Reference]] with its origin at the center of the Earth. Its axes are fixed relative to the distant stars (the [[Celestial Sphere]]), meaning they do not rotate with the Earth.

**Axis Definitions:**
*   **Fundamental Plane:** The Earth's [[equatorial plane]], extended to the [[Celestial Sphere]] (the [[Celestial Equator]]).
*   **Principal Direction (X-axis):** Points from the Earth's center towards the **[[Vernal Equinox]] (γ)**.
*   **Z-axis:** Points from the Earth's center towards the **[[North Celestial Pole]]** (the projection of Earth's rotation axis).
*   **Y-axis:** Completes the right-handed triad, lying in the [[equatorial plane]] 90° east of the [[Vernal Equinox]].

**Key Characteristics:**
*   It is the primary inertial frame used for describing the orbits of Earth-orbiting satellites.
*   Because it is non-rotating, it is the appropriate frame for applying Newton's laws of motion and for propagating orbits.
*   The ECI frame is the direct equivalent of the **Geocentric Equatorial Coordinate System** used in astronomy.
