### **4. Earth-Centered, Earth-Fixed Frame (ECEF)**

**Formal Definition:**
The **Earth-Centered, Earth-Fixed Frame** is a non-inertial, body-fixed reference frame with its origin at the center of the Earth. Its axes are fixed to and rotate with the Earth.

**Axis Definitions:**
*   **Fundamental Plane:** The Earth's [[Equatorial Plane]].
*   **Z-axis:** Coincides with the Earth's axis of rotation, pointing towards the [[Conventional Terrestrial Pole]] (CTP).
*   **X-axis:** Points from the Earth's center towards the intersection of the equator and the **[[Greenwich Meridian]]** (0° [[Geographic Longitude]], passing through Greenwich, England).
*   **Y-axis:** Completes the right-handed triad, pointing towards 90° East [[Geographic Longitude]].

**Key Characteristics:**
*   It is a **rotating frame**. A point on the Earth's surface has fixed (or slowly changing due to plate tectonics) coordinates in this frame.
*   It is essential for all ground-based operations, including mapping, GPS coordinate specification, and relating the position of a satellite to a [[ground station]]'s location.
*   Coordinates are typically given as [[Geodetic Latitude]], [[Geodetic Longitude]], and Height above a reference ellipsoid (like WGS84).
