[[Frame of Reference]]

The **Topocentric Equatorial Reference Frame** is a coordinate system used to specify the position of a celestial object as seen from a specific, fixed location on the [[surface]] of the Earth. Its origin is centered at the **observer's location** (Greek: _topos_, meaning "place"). It shares the same fundamental orientation as the geocentric equatorial system—its axes are aligned with the [[Celestial Equator]] and the [[Vernal Equinox]] ([[First Point of Aries]])—but it is translated from the Earth's center to the observer's position.
